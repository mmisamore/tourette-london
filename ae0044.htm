Compulsions

Compulsions are repetitive, deliberate actions that occur in an attempt to relieve the anxiety caused by obsessions.

Rituals or Ritualistic Behaviours (Compulsions) can be seemingly endless and can occupy hours and even days of a person’s life. The person may feel compelled to perform a certain movement or activity a certain number of times before they can make the transition to a daily activity. Stepping back and forth through a doorway three times before walking into a room or perfectly aligning things on a desk before starting the next assignment are examples of this behaviour.

If the person feels or perceives that the ritual in not correct, they must repeat it from the beginning until it “feels right.” For people who have lengthy rituals it can mean being chronically late for appointments because of time consuming dressing rituals. It can mean never getting assignments done despite sound knowledge of the material because the person can’t get some insignificant detail “right.”

Other examples of compulsions are:

Checking,

Counting,

Touching,

Arranging,

Evening things up,

Picking at self, e.g., skin, scabs, clothing,

Seeking reassurance, e.g., repetitive questioning, and

Hand washing.

Compulsions may occur by themselves or may be part of Obsessive Compulsive Disorder(OCD.)
