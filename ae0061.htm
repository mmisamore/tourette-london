Chronic

Tourette Syndrome is considered a chronic condition because the diagnosis requires that the tics be present for more than 1 year as in the definition of TS shown below.

The accepted medical usage of the word chronic is “a persistent and lasting disease or medical condition, or one that that developed slowly.”

“Tourette Syndrome is a chronic neurochemical disorder that causes involuntary movements and sounds known as tics.”
