Complex Obsessions and Compulsions List

The Tic List lists examples of tics and simple obsessions and compulsions.

The Complex Obsessions and Compulsion List lists examples of more complex obsessions and compulsions.

-

Has a need to finish verbalizations if interrupted. The person may be telling a boring story and someone tries to interrupt but the person must continue until the story is finished. The person may be explaining a process and the person listening already understands the process and tries to interrupt. The person with the obsession must continue until the instructions are complete.

-

Repeats sounds, words, music, etc over and over in head. This can be for short times or can occur repeatedly for days.

-

Is concerned about blurting out obscenities. May avoid speaking in public or in front of a class in case an obscenity or insult is blurted out. May avoid social occasions also. See Coprolalia.

-

Is concerned about harming self or others. May avoid using knives, scissors. May avoid walking past a window in case it may break.

-

Is concerned about germs and becoming sick from contact with people or things. Avoids contact with other people especially shaking hands. Avoids touching objects such as door handles. Avoids sticky, messy or moist objects. Spends and abnormal amount of time washing hands or washes hands more often than would be normal.

-

Seeks assurance by asking questions. Repeats questions over and over, often modifying the question slightly. Often the answer is not reassuring and must ask again and again.

-

Concerned about doing something sinful, doing the right thing or having told a lie. Overly concerned about morality in self or others. Confesses to behaviours he never did. Seeks reassurance he wasn’t’ responsible for an act. Confesses to sins of religious origin. Gets stuck on something someone else did - cannot let go of a perceived injustice.
