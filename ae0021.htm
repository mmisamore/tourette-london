Autism

Autism is a complex developmental disability that affects a personâ€™s ability to communicate and interact with other people. Autism typically appears before the age of three.

Some symptoms include:

- Lack of or delay in spoken language

- Repetitive use of language and/or motor mannerisms (e.g., hand-flapping, twirling objects)

- Little or no eye contact

- Lack of interest in peer relationships

- Lack of spontaneous or make-believe play

- Persistent fixation on parts of objects

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention in the USA, in 2007, reported that the prevalence for Autism is 1 in 150 children.

Autism is one of five disorders that fall under the umbrella of Pervasive Developmental Disorders(PDD).
